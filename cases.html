Cases



Since 31 December 2019 and as of 14 November 2020, 53 515 055 cases of COVID-19 (in accordance with the applied case definitions and testing strategies in the affected countries) have been reported, including 1 304 871 deaths.

Cases have been reported from:

Africa: 1 949 530 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are South Africa (746 945), Morocco (282 336), Egypt (110 319), Ethiopia (101 757) and Tunisia (77 668).

Asia: 14 696 165 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are India (8 773 479), Iran (738 322), Iraq (514 496), Indonesia (457 735) and Bangladesh (428 965).

America: 23 064 809 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are United States (10 739 614), Brazil (5 810 652), Argentina (1 296 363), Colombia (1 182 697) and Mexico (997 393).

Europe: 13 756 317 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are France (1 922 504), Russia (1 880 551), Spain (1 458 591), United Kingdom (1 317 496) and Italy (1 107 303).

Oceania: 47 538 cases; the five countries reporting most cases are Australia (27 703), French Polynesia (11 485), Guam (5 924), New Zealand (1 642) and Papua New Guinea (600).

Other: 696 cases have been reported from an international conveyance in Japan.

Deaths have been reported from:

Africa: 46 829 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are South Africa (20 153), Egypt (6 429), Morocco (4 631), Tunisia (2 209) and Algeria (2 125).

Asia: 260 477 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are India (129 188), Iran (40 582), Indonesia (15 037), Iraq (11 580) and Turkey (11 326).

America: 677 168 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are United States (244 345), Brazil (164 737), Mexico (97 624), Peru (35 106) and Argentina (35 045).

Europe: 319 302 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are United Kingdom (51 304), Italy (44 139), France (43 892), Spain (40 769) and Russia (32 443).

Oceania: 1 088 deaths; the five countries reporting most deaths are Australia (907), Guam (93), French Polynesia (52), New Zealand (25) and Papua New Guinea (7).

Other: 7 deaths have been reported from an international conveyance in Japan.
